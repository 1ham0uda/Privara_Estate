## Clarify Your Objective First

The North Coast means different things to different buyers. Before evaluating any specific project, you need to be honest about your primary objective:

| Objective | What that means for your decision |
|---|---|
| **Lifestyle / personal use** | Location, community, and developer quality matter more than yield math |
| **Capital appreciation** | Buy early in the development cycle in areas with committed infrastructure |
| **Rental income** | Weekly rental yields, management infrastructure, and access matter most |
| **Diversification / USD hedge** | Dollar-denominated projects with solid developer track records |

Many buyers arrive with objective #1 but evaluate using the metrics of objective #3. This mismatch leads to disappointment.

## The Seasonality Problem

The North Coast summer season in Egypt runs approximately **June through September** — 16 weeks. Realistically, a managed vacation rental property achieves 8–12 occupied weeks per year, depending on:

- Quality of the property management company
- Price point relative to comparable units
- Whether the development has a hotel operator managing short-term lets

An annual rental yield of 6–8% on purchase price is achievable at the top end, but this requires active management, competitive pricing, and a well-located unit within a compound that has strong rental demand.

**The default assumption for financial modelling should be 4–6% yield.** If the investment does not make sense at 4%, it requires specific conditions to work.

## Developer Concentration and What It Means

Three groups dominate quality North Coast inventory: Tatweer Misr, Mountain View (DMG), and Ora Developers (Emaar Misr has also delivered in the corridor). This concentration is simultaneously reassuring and a pricing constraint.

The implications:

1. **Brand premium is real but priced in.** These developers command 15–25% premiums over equivalent mid-tier projects. This is justified by track record and exit liquidity — resale in branded projects is materially faster.
2. **Sub-developer risk exists.** Several projects marketed as being within branded master plans are developed by third parties under a license agreement. Understand who your actual counterparty is.
3. **Common area delivery is outside your developer's control** in master-planned zones. The road connecting your compound to the highway may be the master developer's responsibility.

## Practical Questions for Site Visits

If you are visiting a North Coast project:

- Ask to see **delivered** units in the same project, not just show units.
- Walk the **beachfront access** route — is it paved, lit, and functional, or theoretical?
- Ask which **hotel brand or management company** runs the rental pool, and request their occupancy data for the previous two seasons.
- Verify the **electricity connection type** (temporary generator vs. national grid connection).
- Understand the **service charge structure** — North Coast service charges range from EGP 300 to 900 per sqm per year and have increased significantly since 2022.
