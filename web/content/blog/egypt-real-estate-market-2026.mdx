## The Macro Picture

Egypt's real estate market in 2026 is defined by three competing forces: sustained demand for residential units driven by population growth, a wave of new off-plan supply from large developers, and currency-adjusted pricing that has made some segments globally competitive for foreign buyers.

The Egyptian pound's partial stabilisation since 2024 has reduced the volatility premium that buyers were pricing in. However, **real returns in EGP terms remain below historical averages**, meaning buyers seeking yield rather than capital preservation need to be selective.

## Off-Plan: Volume vs. Value

Developer pipelines are historically full. New Cairo, Sheikh Zayed, and the New Administrative Capital together account for an estimated **85,000 units** scheduled for delivery between 2026 and 2028. This is not uniformly a risk — but it does mean:

- **Completion risk is real.** Some mid-tier developers are behind schedule by 12–18 months.
- **Phase-one pricing advantages have largely been extracted.** Buyers entering now in phase two or three of projects face narrower margins.
- **Quality divergence is widening.** The gap between the top-five developers and the rest is measurable in finish quality, facilities delivery, and aftersales service.

Our recommendation: buyers considering off-plan should commission an independent financial model — not rely on developer-provided projections.

## Resale: Where the Real Opportunities Are

The resale market, often overshadowed by new launches, currently offers some of the most asymmetric risk-reward scenarios available. Motivated sellers in areas like Maadi, Zamalek, and established New Cairo compounds are pricing at discounts to replacement cost.

Key patterns we observe:

1. **Inherited properties** are entering the market at below-market prices, particularly in Old Cairo and Alexandria.
2. **Upgraders** — families moving from apartments to villas — are creating liquidity in mid-tier apartment segments.
3. **Distressed off-plan resales** from buyers who over-committed during the 2022–2023 boom are available at meaningful discounts.

## The North Coast Question

The North Coast remains one of Egypt's most debated real estate sub-markets. Our view, based on client consultations and market data:

- **Seasonal utility** limits yield for buyers who want year-round rental income. North Coast properties typically achieve occupancy of 6–10 weeks per year in the rental market.
- **Developer concentration** creates counterparty risk. Three developers control the majority of quality inventory, which is both a signal of credibility and a source of pricing power.
- **Dollar-denominated pricing** in some projects creates a natural hedge for USD earners but an FX mismatch for EGP buyers.
