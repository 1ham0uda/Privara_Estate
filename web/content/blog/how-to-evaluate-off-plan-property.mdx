## The Information Asymmetry Problem

When you attend an off-plan launch event, you are receiving information curated by a party with a direct financial interest in your decision. This does not make developers dishonest — it makes them salespeople. The job of an independent advisor is to restore information symmetry.

The questions below are the ones our consultants ask on every off-plan mandate. Use them as your starting checklist.

## Developer Track Record

**Questions to ask, evidence to demand:**

- How many units have you delivered in the last five years? (Ask for a specific list, not a marketing figure.)
- What was the variance between the promised and actual delivery date on your last three projects?
- Can you provide contact details for the residents' committee of a completed project?
- Is your company publicly listed? If not, who are your audited shareholders?

A developer who resists these questions is providing important information by doing so.

## The Financial Model

**Never accept a developer's projected return.** Build your own:

1. **Purchase price** — Total, including registration, maintenance deposits, and installment financing costs if applicable.
2. **Delivery date realism** — Add 12 months to stated delivery as a conservative assumption.
3. **Rental yield** — Research comparable delivered units in the same area, not the same project. Apply a 70–75% occupancy rate for a realistic figure.
4. **Capital appreciation** — Use CPI + real growth assumptions, not developer-cited "up to X%" figures.
5. **Exit liquidity** — How actively traded is resale in this project/area? Ask a neutral broker, not the developer's sales team.

## Location Due Diligence

Off-plan is a bet on what a location will become, not what it is. Ask:

- What infrastructure is committed vs. announced? (Committed = funded + under construction.)
- What is the demographic profile of buyers in this project? (Mixed end-user/investor vs. predominantly investor suggests higher vacancy risk on delivery.)
- What is the developer's relationship with the surrounding master developer? (Sub-developers in large master plans have less control over common area delivery timelines.)

## Contract Review

Before signing any purchase contract, verify:

- **Price fixation:** Is the price per square meter fixed, or can it be adjusted before delivery?
- **Specification schedule:** Is the unit specification attached to the contract as a schedule, or referenced by reference only?
- **Penalty clauses:** What penalties apply to the developer for late delivery? (Egyptian courts do enforce these, but the amount matters.)
- **Cancellation terms:** Under what conditions can you exit, and what is the cost?
